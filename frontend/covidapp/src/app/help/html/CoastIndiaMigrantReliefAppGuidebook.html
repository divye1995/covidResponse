CoastIndia Migrant Relief App Guidebook

 

Introduction:

This initiative was born as a COVID response of the NGO WASSAN to help stranded migrant workers in Telengana and Andhra. The initial app was then further developed with the support of Azim Premji Philanthropic Initiatives to have a nation-wide reach. The migrant workers’ data was provided by the Jharkhand government, collected through the helpline since the lockdown.

Basic Instructions to get you started:

Our aim is to ascertain the needs of stranded people and try to ensure that they have access to food, shelter, health services (if needed), and money (in this order of priority). If they do not have access, do your best to direct them to resources already listed in your locality. You have to search for these resources.

Unfortunately, we do not have full knowledge of all the resources available on the ground to guide our beneficiaries to them. Hence we are setting up google groups with a leader for each district. The google group then becomes a peer learning group. Pls. share tips and resources you discover with your group. Also check the Resources listed at the end of this doc.

Each volunteer (including leader) are assigned a few (10-20) cases. We are targeting a minimum of 3 calls/day

Objective for all volunteers- Reach out to each case, verify recorded data, ascertain current needs, try to meet needs, update the case data on the app and hit submit form after call.

  • To update needs, please check and uncheck boxes
  • Fill in the Urgency column: (High  - Medical Needs/no food and or no shelter/Transportation Home; Med - no food/shelter;  Low - no cash or needs transportation; Not needed--all boxes in Help Requested are unchecked.);

Fill in the Status column: Close case once you have determined that Urgency = Not needed.

In order to support you all better, we have now arranged for Zoom support calls on a daily basis with experienced WASSAN staff to answer any (non-technical) question you may have about being the first line of response to a migrant worker in distress. Support to using the app is also provided by the tech team.

Using the Coast India Application

Users:

Volunteers primarily use this application to contact the stranded migrant workers on-ground and fill in various details on the application.

Admins utilize the application to assign cases to volunteers and create/update the complete list of organisations working in their given state.

Accessing the Application

Step 1:

Log onto https://admin.coastindia.org/ to access the application.

Step 2:

You can log in using your Google/Facebook account

Note (For Volunteers): Once you have logged in, email bindu@auroville.org.in with the Subject: Registered as a Volunteer

We recommend using computers since some are reporting interface challenges with mobile.

Step 3:

Once you log in, you can access the various features on the homepage.

About: Click to know more about the work and functioning of the Coast India Platform.

Add Help Seeker: Click to add a migrant worker or a person who might need any sort of assistance.

Add Support Network: Click to add an organisation involved in assisting migrant workers in some part of the country.

My List: If you’re a volunteer, you can access your assigned cases through this tab.

Full List: If you’re an admin, you can access the complete list of your organisation through this tab.

Logout: Click to log out from your account.

 

Filling and Updating Relief Form (for Volunteers)

Step 1:

Volunteers will have been assigned certain case(s) that can be viewed on clicking the “My List” tab.

Step 2:

If multiple cases are assigned, you can look for a given organisation in the search field using the multiple filters provided such as Status and Urgency.

Step 3:

The address of the organisation and the phone number of the contact person is mentioned in the list.  

Step 4:

After having contacted the person in charge, you are needed to fill in details on the application. To do so, click on the “Edit” button under Actions. You will be directed to “form updating window”.

Step 5:

Please fill in the required details on the form based on your interactions with the contact-person. The fields marked with red asterisk (Urgency, Status, Notes) are mandatory.

Side Note: On this page, you will notice a “History” section at top which will list the previous forms that you filled and edited.

Step 6:

There are multiple tabs in the form. Once you have completed filling one section, click on “Next” to move to the next one. Fill in the details wherever necessary.

Fill the Current Needs to determine the wide spectrum of requirements and assistance that needs to be provided.

Fill Contact info with the details of the help seeker.

Note: It is important to fill in the address details with as much accuracy as possible.

Fill Where From to allow us to figure out the transportation of the migrant workers back to their native districts. This is important.

Fill Cash Transfers if there is a need for monetary support. We are using this option minimally.

Fill Form filler info to leave your contact details and add additional notes.

Save Details to submit the form.

Step 7:

Once you have reached the last tab “Save Details”, Click on “Submit Form” to complete the submission process. A submitted form reflects in the History tab.

Note: Please note that the form is only submitted once you click this otherwise all data that you have entered will be lost.

Step 8:

Please keep on updating this form as and when the case progresses with relevant information such as “Help already Provided”. You may also need to include “local help” who provided the assistance under the current needs tab.

Note: If the organisation that provided the assistance isn’t mentioned in the list, you can add it by clicking on “Add Support Network” on the main tab and then selecting the added organisation from the list.

Assigning Cases to volunteers (for admins)

Step 1:

Click on the full list tab to access the complete list of your assigned state.

Step 2:

Use the search tab to search for a specific organisation in your state. You can use the various filters provided to assist the process. Select “Help Seekers” under the Type filter to get a list of individuals in need of assistance that need to be assigned to volunteers.

Note: Super-admins can search across states by using the Group filter.

Step 3:

To assign cases based on volunteer expertise, check the given boxes for a more specific list.

Step 4:

Select “Unassigned” to obtain a list of cases that haven’t been assigned to any volunteers.

Step 5:

Select the cases you wish to assign to a volunteer. You can select all cases by checking the box placed next to the Name tab.

Step 6:

Once you select the cases, an “Assign to volunteer” tab appears. Click on it to select the volunteer from a drop-down list.

Note: If you are a super-admin, you might get the option to assign the cases to an entire group.

Step 7:

Keep checking the complete list filtered through open and urgent cases to keep a tab on the progress of action in your assigned state.
